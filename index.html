Welcome to Mr. Mannings Coding Class

I am not here for a paycheck, and you are not here for a grade. We are here to develop the ability to communicate with computers. This is no easy task. You have to learn how to think like a computer. You have a lot of vocabulary and rules to learn. You must become extremely detail orientated. And you must learn to overcome the frustration of failure that is a part of developing any significant program. Becoming a computer programmer is a long journey, but one worth taking. With our abilities we can create games and art, we can solve problems, and we can hack the technological world around us.

- Mr. Manning

Grading

A working day is a day that you are present in class for at least half the duration, and have access to a working computer, and have internet access. Every working day I expect you to complete 1 section of a unit (script kiddie, programmer, or hacker). So every 3 working days you should be turning in a unit through edmodo.

The algorithm for your grade is the number of units you have completed, divided by your number of working days divided by 3(rounded down). This algorithm can be adjusted on a case by case basis.

If your grade drops below a 70% you will be placed on probation. Which means you no longer get to choose your own seat. You will sit near me and I will make sure you stay focused on coding.
